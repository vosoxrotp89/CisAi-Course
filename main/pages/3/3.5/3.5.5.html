Activity 3: Traffic Considerations

Your trading company now boasts a fully functional back-end network that is optimized for AI workloads, and everything is running smoothly. In fact, the performance has been so impressive that the HPC team has taken notice. Your company observed that the speeds and data throughput in the AI network are significantly better than those in their own data center, which is rather old and ready for a revamp. Impressed by this performance, the HPC team is now considering moving their servers into the AI data center to take advantage of its efficiency and capabilities. However, they are also concerned about the potential risks and challenges of integrating HPC workloads with AI workloads.

Content Review Question

Incorrect

Given the conflicting demands of these workloads, which two of the following considerations must you make to ensure that both AI and HPC workloads perform optimally on the same network? (Choose two.)

Answer

The correct answers answers are Prioritize HPC traffic using Quality of Service (QoS) policies to ensure that HPC data is transmitted with minimal latency and does not get stuck behind AI-related traffic in the network queues and Disable PFC for HPC traffic to avoid latency spikes caused by pause frames, as HPC workloads cannot tolerate delays. HPC workloads are typically very sensitive to latency. Implementing QoS policies to prioritize HPC traffic ensures that these workloads are processed with minimal delay, which prevents them from being affected by AI traffic congestion. Disabling PFC for HPC traffic helps avoid the latency spikes caused by pause frames. HPC workloads are highly sensitive to latency, and avoiding these delays is crucial for maintaining optimal performance.

The answer Use PFC for both AI and HPC traffic to ensure that neither workload experiences packet loss, as lossless transmission is crucial for both is incorrect. While PFC is beneficial for AI workloads to ensure lossless communication, it introduces latency due to pause frames, which is detrimental to latency-sensitive HPC workloads. Therefore, PFC should be used for AI but disabled for HPC.

The answer Use ECN as the primary congestion control mechanism for HPC traffic to reduce latency and ensure that data is transmitted as quickly as possible is incorrect. ECN helps manage congestion but does not sufficiently address the latency requirements of HPC workloads. It signals the sender to slow down, which can still introduce delays. HPC traffic requires a different approach, such as disabling PFC and using best-effort paths.

The answer Implement redundant network paths for both AI and HPC traffic to ensure high availability and failover capabilities is incorrect. While redundancy is important for network resilience, it does not directly address the performance optimization for AI and HPC workloads on the same network.

Content Review Question

Incorrect

Your colleague is confused. Why are both PFC and ECN essential for managing AI workloads in RoCEv2 environments?

Answer

The correct answer is PFC ensures a lossless network by pausing traffic during congestion, while ECN proactively marks packets to adjust transmission rates before severe congestion occurs, preventing packet loss and minimizing pauses. PFC guarantees lossless transport by pausing specific traffic and ECN helps by marking packets to reduce transmission rates before congestion becomes severe. This approach allows for smoother traffic flow and prevents excessive pauses.

The answer PFC ensures that congestion is avoided by slowing down the entire network, while ECN marks packets that need to be dropped to prevent packet loss is incorrect. PFC does not slow down the entire network; it pauses specific traffic classes to ensure no packet loss. ECN marks packets to notify the sender to reduce the transmission rate and avoid packet drops.

The answer PFC and ECN both work by dropping low-priority packets when congestion is detected, ensuring high-priority traffic is transmitted without delay is incorrect. Neither PFC nor ECN drops packets. PFC pauses specific traffic classes to prevent packet loss, and ECN marks packets to reduce the sender's transmission rate and prevent congestion without dropping packets.

The answer PFC works on small-scale networks by controlling congestion, while ECN is only needed in large AI clusters to manage multiple nodes communicating at the same time is incorrect. Both PFC and ECN are needed in networks of all sizes. They ensure lossless transport (through PFC) and efficient congestion management (through ECN) in AI workloads, regardless of the scale of the cluster.
