Cost Optimization

In early 2024, UPS made an announcement that caused concern for many people. To control rising costs, UPS decided to lay off 12,000 people and replace them with AI-enabled systems. Many fear this trend will continue with potentially harmful results. Others believe it is just a shift in the workforce environment, much like the introduction of the loom back in the early 1800s. Regardless of how you view this shift, one thing is for certain: companies are looking to use AI to optimize operating costs.

And while AI can help organizations reduce operating costs, it can also be quite expensive to implement. Many factors affect AI costs, such as infrastructure, energy, training, and development. Also, there are human capital costs. The proper deployment and use of AI requires specialized skills that currently demand a high wage.

Due to all these factors, before implementing an AI strategy, it is important that you consider actions that minimize the cost of an AI implementation. AI costs normally fit into five categories: data, compute, development, utilization, and maintenance.

Data Costs

The acquisition and processing required to produce high-quality data are sometimes not considered when organizations first decide to explore an AI solution. When most people use a text-generation AI service such as OpenAI, Claude, Bard, or one of the other numerous AI chat services, they dont stop to consider the massive amount of time, energy, and cost that went into creating these systems. It is estimated that the cost of developing OpenAIs ChatGPT is over 100 million dollars. It is expected that these costs will continue to increase with some predicting them to reach over 100 billion dollars (about $310 per person in the US).

Fortunately, organizations may not need to build and train entire AI models because they can often fine-tune pre-trained models to their specific use case. For example, effective AI requires high-quality, clean data. Raw data must be cleaned either manually, by using automation, by employing a service, or perhaps a combination of these methods. Preparing accurate, reliable, and clean data will facilitate fine-tuning an existing model, ensuring that it generates quality inferences, increases efficiency and reduces overall cost.

Compute Costs

Another major consideration of AI is the cost of the computing power that it requires. One of the first decisions organizations that are implementing AI need to make is whether to use an on-prem or cloud computing solution. Each solution has its benefits and drawbacks so strong consideration should be given to both.

When determining which type of deployment will be the most cost-effective, you also need to balance that decision with your organization's other requirements. For example, if you want to use a cloud solution because it is the most cost-effective solution given your expected utilization, but your company has a policy that no internal information can be stored on third-party servers, perhaps a hybrid solution would be most appropriate.

Development Costs

When deciding on an AI development path, you have essentially two choices: a pre-built or a custom solution. The costs associated with these options can vary significantly based on several factors. Development costs encompass not only the financial investment but also the time, resources, and potential risks involved in creating and maintaining an AI solution. These costs are crucial because they directly impact the overall effectiveness and sustainability of the AI deployment.

Key questions that should guide the evaluation of development costs include:

  • What is the complexity of the AI system? More complex systems require more development time and expertise, which can drive up costs.

  • How much customization is needed? The degree of customization will affect both the initial and ongoing costs.

  • What is the size of the dataset? Larger datasets may require more robust infrastructure, influencing cost considerations.

  • What are the scalability requirements? The ability of the AI solution to scale with the organizations needs can have significant cost implications.

  • What are the maintenance and update requirements? Ongoing maintenance and the need for regular updates can also impact the total cost of ownership.

Understanding and defining these costs is vital for making an informed decision between a pre-built or custom AI solution. Development costs should be viewed as a parameter that influences the design and deployment of AI systems, ensuring they align with the organizations budget and long-term goals.

Utilization Costs

AI utilization costs, the expenses incurred during the inference phase of AI models, can sometimes surpass all other associated costs, particularly when using cloud AI services. These cloud services typically charge based on the number of tokens processed by your AI solution. Some providers offer flat monthly fees with a cap on total usage, which are usually designed for solutions with anticipated lower utilization.

The following figure shows the costs associated with different versions of OpenAI's GPT-4 model. GPT-4 is a widely used language model that powers various applications, from chatbots to content generation tools. The different versions in the figure reflect varying capabilities and costs, with higher-end models like GPT-4-32k offering more extensive features at a higher price point.

The good news is that as models and hardware improve, the cost per token is decreasing. For reference, A prompt token is a unit of text input processed by the model; more detailed or complex outputs will generally use more tokens. OpenAIs GPT-4-32k currently costs $60 per 1 million prompt tokens, whereas GPT-4o costs only $5 per million prompt tokens.

Choosing the correct model for your needs can have a serious impact on the overall cost of the solution.

Maintenance Costs

AI maintenance costs often go hand in hand with the development path that you choose. Prebuilt solutions often include maintenance in the plan, so the cost is already included, and it is predictable. However, custom solutions can often incur additional costs for things like dataset refreshes, model upgrades, and feature add-ons, just to name a few. A custom-built solution, also requires a budget to be allocated for maintenance costs, ensuring the system remains functional, up-to-date, and capable of meeting evolving needs.
