Hypervisor

A hypervisor, also known as a VMM, is a software layer that enables the creation, management, and operation of VMs by abstracting the underlying physical hardware. Hypervisors play an important role in virtualization by allowing multiple operating systems to run simultaneously on a single physical machine, each within its isolated VM.

There are two types of hypervisors.

The key functions of hypervisors are as follows:

  • Resource allocation: Hypervisors allocate and manage physical resources (CPU, memory, storage, network) among the VMs to ensure efficient utilization and prevent conflicts.

  • Isolation: Each VM operates in a separate, isolated environment, which enhances security and stability by preventing issues in one VM from affecting others.

  • Portability: VMs can be easily moved or copied between different physical hosts to facilitate load balancing, maintenance, and disaster recovery.

  • Scalability: Hypervisors allow for easy scaling of virtual environments by adding or removing VMs as needed without significant changes to the underlying hardware.
